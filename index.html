Welcome and thankyou for your interest in Soundkiss

Coloured Whale in session at Soundkiss 2014

This is a legacy web page for Soundkiss, a recording studio I established and ran in Taipei, Taiwan ROC from 2011-2015.

The same day we closed I had to rush to the airport to catch a flight home to London, and so ended 8 years of live in Taiwan. Only when the plane was taking off did I fully realise everything I was leaving behind.

Years on and many more gigs, recordings and tours under my belt, I still remember that other life. The four years running Soundkiss were some of the best with the sessions, friendships and records we made.

This page is my record.


Background

Having trained in music production and had years playing in bands then working in live sound in the UK, when I travelled to Taiwan in 2008 to pursue Mandarin studies, I was immediately drawn to Taipeiâ€™s vibrant music scene.

Alex 2012

For a small island, there was so much going on. I was soon playing out in bands myself and took up foh residencies at several local venues

I became known to the music community for my commitment to my work and was increasingly asked if I would record.

With this encouragement, wheels in motion I started thinking that a recording studio of my design could be more than an improbable dream, and actually a reality.

From the outset, I shared my ideas with Crystal Shien, who as things got rolling enthusiastically joined me as my Taiwanese business partner, and would become integral to making the place and our sessions work.

Crystal 2014

I looked for several months for a suitable space to get building in. Finally in the Zhongshan district of central Taipei, I found one.

On the 6th floor of an old commercial building featuring a retro elevator, we created a soundproofed space featuring a live room large enough for bands to track together, a control room and several other adaptive spaces.

I found ways to get natural reverbs from the lift shaft, the bathroom or a homemade plate reverb in the manner of recording studios from yesteryear.

We could reposition the drum kit, instruments and microphones on rolling stock. Bands could sit down and eat something together, have a smoke on the balcony, or take a breather in the nearby park.

Located in a party district with cinemas, hotels, stores, restaurants and karaoke bars just outside, before and after session there were plenty of options. The studio was quiet to work in too and full of natural light.

I used to love at quiet times taking a breather on the empty roof space above and looking out over adjacent Kangle Park.
